> “From French essence, from Latin essentia (“the being or essence of a thing”), from an artificial formation of esse (“to be”), to translate Ancient Greek οὐσία (ousía, “being”), from ὤν (ṓn), present participle of εἰμί (eimí, “I am, exist”).” – [Etymology]([https://www.etymonline.com/search?page=1&q=essence](https://www.etymonline.com/search?page=1&q=essence))
